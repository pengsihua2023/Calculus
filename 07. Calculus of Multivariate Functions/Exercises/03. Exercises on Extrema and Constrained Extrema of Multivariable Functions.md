## **1. Stationary Point & Classification**

**Problem:** Find and classify the stationary points of

$$
f(x,y)=x^2+y^2-2x-4y.
$$

**Solution:**

$$
f_x=2x-2,\quad f_y=2y-4.
$$

Stationary point: $2x-2=0\Rightarrow x=1$, $2y-4=0\Rightarrow y=2$.

Second derivatives: $f_{xx}=2,\ f_{yy}=2,\ f_{xy}=0.$

$$
D=f_{xx}f_{yy}-(f_{xy})^2=4>0,\ f_{xx}=2>0\Rightarrow \text{minimum}.
$$

Value: $f(1,2)=1+4-2-8=-5.$

âœ… **Answer:** Minimum at $(1,2)$, value $-5$.

## **2. Saddle Point Example**

**Problem:** Determine extrema of

$$
f(x,y)=x^2-y^2.
$$

**Solution:**

$$
f_x=2x,\ f_y=-2y.
$$

Stationary point: $(0,0).$
Second derivatives: $f_{xx}=2,\ f_{yy}=-2,\ f_{xy}=0.$

$$
D=2\cdot(-2)-0=-4<0 \quad \Rightarrow \text{saddle point}.
$$

âœ… **Answer:** $(0,0)$ is a saddle point (no max/min).

## **3. Unconstrained Maximum**

**Problem:** Find max/min of

$$
f(x,y)=-(x^2+y^2)+4x+6y.
$$

**Solution:**

$$
f_x=-2x+4,\ f_y=-2y+6.
$$

Stationary point: $x=2,\ y=3.$
Second derivatives: $f_{xx}=-2,\ f_{yy}=-2,\ f_{xy}=0.$

$$
D=(-2)(-2)-0=4>0,\ f_{xx}<0 \Rightarrow \text{maximum}.
$$

Value: $f(2,3)=-(4+9)+8+18=13.$

âœ… **Answer:** Maximum at (2,3), value = 13.

## **4. Constrained Minimum (Line Constraint)**

**Problem:** Find extrema of

$$
f(x,y)=x^2+y^2
$$

subject to $x+y=4$.

**Solution (Lagrange multipliers):**

$$
L=x^2+y^2+\lambda(x+y-4).
$$

Equations:

$$
2x+\lambda=0,\quad 2y+\lambda=0,\quad x+y=4.
$$

From first two: $2x=2y\Rightarrow x=y.$
Constraint: $2x=4\Rightarrow x=y=2.$
Value: $f(2,2)=8.$

âœ… **Answer:** Minimum at (2,2), value 8.

## **5. Circle Constraint**

**Problem:** Find extrema of

$$
f(x,y)=x+y
$$

on circle $x^2+y^2=1$.

**Solution:**

$$
L=x+y+\lambda(x^2+y^2-1).
$$

Equations:

$$
1+2\lambda x=0,\ 1+2\lambda y=0,\ x^2+y^2=1.
$$

So $x=y,$ and $1+2\lambda x=0 \Rightarrow \lambda=-\tfrac{1}{2x}.$
Constraint: $2x^2=1\Rightarrow x=\pm\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}},\ y=\pm\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}.$
At $(\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}},\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}})$, value $=\sqrt{2}$.
At $(-1/\sqrt{2},-1/\sqrt{2})$, value $=-\sqrt{2}$.

âœ… **Answer:** Maximum = $\sqrt{2}$, minimum = $-\sqrt{2}$.

## **6. Ellipse Constraint**

**Problem:** Maximize $f(x,y)=x^2y$ subject to $\frac{x^2}{4}+y^2=1$.

**Solution:**

$$
L=x^2y+\lambda\left(\frac{x^2}{4}+y^2-1\right).
$$

$$
L_x=2xy+\frac{\lambda}{2}x=0,\quad L_y=x^2+2\lambda y=0.
$$

Case 1: $x=0\Rightarrow y=\pm1\Rightarrow f=0.$
Case 2: $x\neq 0$. From first: $2y+\frac{\lambda}{2}=0\Rightarrow \lambda=-4y.$
Plug in second: $x^2+2(-4y)y=x^2-8y^2=0\Rightarrow x^2=8y^2.$
Constraint: $\tfrac{8y^2}{4}+y^2=2y^2+ y^2=3y^2=1\Rightarrow y^2=\tfrac{1}{3},\ x^2=\tfrac{8}{3}.$
So candidates: $(\pm\sqrt{8/3}, \pm 1/\sqrt{3}).$
Function values: $f=x^2y=\tfrac{8}{3}(\pm 1/\sqrt{3})=\pm \tfrac{8}{3\sqrt{3}}.$

âœ… **Answer:** Maximum $= \tfrac{8}{3\sqrt{3}},$ minimum $=-\tfrac{8}{3\sqrt{3}}.$

## **7. Quadratic Form Test**

**Problem:** Classify the critical point at (0,0) of

$$
f(x,y)=x^2+2xy+3y^2.
$$

**Solution:**

$$
f_{xx}=2,\ f_{yy}=6,\ f_{xy}=2.
$$

$$
D=f_{xx}f_{yy}-f_{xy}^2=2\cdot6-4=8>0,\ f_{xx}=2>0.
$$

So **minimum** at (0,0).

âœ… **Answer:** Minimum at (0,0).

## **8. Box Constraint**

**Problem:** Find maximum and minimum of $f(x,y)=xy$ on square region $[0,1]\times[0,1]$.

**Solution:**
Interior: $f_x=y,\ f_y=x$. Critical point at (0,0), value=0.

On edges:

* $x=0$ â‡’ $f=0$.
* $y=0$ â‡’ $f=0$.
* $x=1$ â‡’ $f=y,\ 0\le y\le1\Rightarrow [0,1].$
* $y=1$ â‡’ $f=x,\ 0\le x\le1\Rightarrow [0,1].$

So min=0, max=1.

âœ… **Answer:** Minimum=0, maximum=1.

## **9. Constrained Optimization with Two Constraints**

**Problem:** Maximize $f(x,y,z)=x+y+z$ subject to

$$
x^2+y^2+z^2=1,\quad x=y.
$$

**Solution:**
Constraint $x=y$. Substitute: function = $2x+z$, constraint: $2x^2+z^2=1$.

So maximize $2x+z$. This is like maximizing linear function subject to ellipse. Use Cauchyâ€“Schwarz:

$$
2x+z \le \sqrt{2x^2+z^2}\cdot \sqrt{2^2+1^2}=\sqrt{1}\cdot \sqrt{5}=\sqrt{5}.
$$

âœ… **Answer:** Maximum = $\sqrt{5}$, minimum = $-\sqrt{5}$.

## **10. Application: Economics**

**Problem:** A firm produces output

$$
Q(x,y)=x^{1/2}y^{1/2}
$$

with budget constraint $x+2y=100$. Find input allocation that maximizes $Q$.

**Solution (Lagrange multipliers):**

$$
L=x^{1/2}y^{1/2}+\lambda(100-x-2y).
$$

$$
L_x=\tfrac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}y^{1/2}-\lambda=0,\quad L_y=\tfrac{1}{2}x^{1/2}y^{-1/2}-2\lambda=0.
$$

From first: $\lambda=\frac{1}{2}\frac{y^{1/2}}{x^{1/2}}.$
From second: $\lambda=\tfrac{1}{4}\frac{x^{1/2}}{y^{1/2}}.$
Equating: $\tfrac{1}{2}\frac{y}{x}=\tfrac{1}{4}\frac{x}{y}\Rightarrow 2y^2=x^2\Rightarrow x=\sqrt{2}y.$
Constraint: $x+2y=100\Rightarrow \sqrt{2}y+2y=100\Rightarrow y=\tfrac{100}{2+\sqrt{2}},\ x=\sqrt{2}y.$

âœ… **Answer:** Optimal allocation $x=\tfrac{100\sqrt{2}}{2+\sqrt{2}},\ y=\tfrac{100}{2+\sqrt{2}}.$
